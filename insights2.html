OFF-PLAN IN KENYA

BRIEFLY ABOUT US

Buying off-plan in Kenya can be a lucrative investment opportunity, but it comes with its own set of risks and considerations. Here are five tips to help you navigate the process effectively: Research the Developer: Before committing to any off-plan purchase, thoroughly research the reputation and track record of the developer. Look into their past projects, reviews from previous buyers, and any legal or financial issues they might have encountered. A reputable developer with a history of successful projects is more likely to deliver on their promises. Understand the Payment Structure: Off-plan purchases typically involve a payment plan spread out over the construction period. Understand the payment structure, including the initial deposit, milestone payments, and final installment. Ensure that the payment schedule is reasonable and manageable for your financial situation. Be wary of developers who demand large upfront payments without adequate safeguards in place. Review the Contract Thoroughly: Carefully review the sales contract provided by the developer before signing anything. Pay close attention to clauses related to completion timelines, penalties for delays, specifications of the property, and dispute resolution mechanisms. Seek legal advice if necessary to ensure that the contract protects your interests and rights as a buyer. Visit the Site and Showroom: Take the time to visit the site of the proposed development and any showroom or model units available. This allows you to visualize the project, assess its location, and get a feel for the quality of construction and finishes. Ask questions about amenities, infrastructure, and potential future developments in the area to gauge the long-term value of the property.
